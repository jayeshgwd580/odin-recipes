How to make Chakali

Description:-

Chakli It is a deep fried savory snack in the shape of spiral with small spikes.



Ingredients of Chakali:-

  • Flour - Rice flour and besan (gram flour or chickpea flour)
  • Spices - red chili powder, coriander powder, turmeric, hing (asafoetida), and salt.
  • Seeds - carom seeds (ajwain), cumin seeds (zeera), and white sesame seeds (til)
  • Fat - Oil, ghee, or butter. This helps make the chakli crispy. Always add hot. I've used ghee.
  • Oil - for frying. I prefer using peanut oil.
  • Water - Use hot water for making the dough.
  • Chakli press - This is the tool we will use to make/shape the chaklis. I got mine from India but linking a similar kind like mine Brass chakli press or a stainless steel chakli press.


Directions:-

  1. In a large mixing bowl, sift the rice flour and gram flour.
  2. To it, add the spices (red chili powder, coriander powder, turmeric, hing (asafoetida), and salt) and seeds (carom seeds (ajwain), cumin seeds (zeera), and white sesame seeds (til)). Mix with a spoon (or spatula) to combine.
  3. Heat ghee (or oil) on stove top or microwave and add to this dry mix.
  4. Incorporate the ghee(oil) into the flour using hands or spoon. It will form a crumbly texture and the color will change to orangish-yellow.
  5. Boil some water and use this hot water, some at a time knead a soft pliable dough. Used approximately half cup hot water.
  6. Rest for 5-10 mins by covering with a wet cloth. Donâ€™t rest for too long as it may get dry.
  7. Give the rested dough a quick knead one more time and then divide into two equal portions (portions can vary depending on the size of your chakli maker)
  8. Take the chakli press and add the star disk to it so it's flat and seals the bottom.
  9. Add one portion of the dough, press so it fits inside the tube. Close and tightly secure the lid/handle of the chakli maker.
  10. Line a plate or baking sheet with parchment paper or cut individual parchment paper squares for each chakali. This makes transferring to oil easy.
  11. Press the chakli press to squeeze out the dough and then move in a circular motion to make spirals.
  12. Make about 4-5 spirals, close to each other for each chakli.
  13. Break the dough and seal the outside and inside edge (i.e start and end) to prevent them from opening while frying.
  14. Continue pressing the remaining chakalis in a similar manner. Keep them covered with a wet cloth to prevent drying. Makes 7-8 chaklis of 4-5 round each with one portion.


TO FRY:-

  1. Heat oil in a frying pan and test the temperature by adding a small piece of the dough. It should rise up gradually in a few seconds.
  2. If it rises too fast then the oil is very hot and if it doesn't then the oil needs to heat up more.
  3. Line a baking tray or plate with paper towel and keep it ready.
  4. Add 1-2 pieces of chakali or as much the pan can hold without over crowding.
  5. Fry on medium-low heat until golden.
  6. Drain and then transfer on an on lined plate to drain excess oil. Keep frying the remaining chaklis in a similar manner.
  7. Crispy and crunchy chaklis are ready!!
